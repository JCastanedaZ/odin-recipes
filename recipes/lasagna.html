World's Best Lasagna



Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of stacked layers of pasta, sauce, and various fillings such as meat, vegetables, cheese, and herbs. The dish is typically baked in the oven until the pasta is cooked and the cheese on top is melted and bubbly.

The traditional lasagna recipe includes wide, flat pasta sheets made from wheat flour and eggs, boiled until they are al dente. The pasta sheets are then layered with tomato sauce, ground beef, and ricotta cheese, and topped with mozzarella cheese. Some recipes also include additional layers of vegetables, such as spinach or zucchini, or different types of cheeses, such as Parmesan or provolone.

  • Onion and garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic are cooked with the meat to add tons of flavor.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.
  • Tomato products: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce.
  • Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce

  • Lasagna noodles: Use store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
